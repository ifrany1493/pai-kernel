# Code of Conduct

## PAI-Kernel · PAI Constitutional Document (PAI-CD)

**Version:** 1.0 · March 2026

---

## Our Commitment

PAI-Kernel exists to establish governance infrastructure that protects human authorial sovereignty from AI systems operating against human interests.

We hold ourselves to the same standard we require of the systems we govern: **no coercion, no manipulation, no undisclosed objectives.**

All participants in this project — contributors, reviewers, and community members — are expected to engage as authors of their own thinking, not as followers of social pressure.

---

## Standards

### Expected behavior

- Engage with arguments, not identities.
- Substantive critique of specifications, invariants, and procedures is not only permitted — it is valued. This is how the standard gets stronger.
- Identify the weakest point in your own argument before presenting it.
- If you disagree with a decision, say so explicitly and explain why. Record your disagreement. Consensus is not required.
- Treat ambiguity as a signal to clarify — not as a license to interpret in your favor.

### Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, harassment, or targeting based on identity.
- Attempting to influence decisions through social pressure, repetition, or emotional leverage rather than argument.
- Misrepresenting others' positions.
- Using authorial language of the project to advance interests outside the project's stated mission.
- Any behavior that would constitute a PAI-CD violation if performed by an AI system toward a human Author.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and any communications conducted in the name of PAI-Kernel.

---

## Enforcement

Reports of unacceptable behavior may be submitted via GitHub Issues (marked `[conduct]`) or by email to `contact@paikernel.org`.

All reports will be reviewed. Actions may include warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent removal from project spaces.

Enforcement decisions are logged and subject to the same transparency standards as all project governance decisions.

---

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is grounded in the normative principles of PAI-CD v2.2 — in particular, Cognitive Sovereignty (freedom from covert manipulation) and Authorship Supremacy (decisions originate from genuine human judgment, not engineered compliance).
